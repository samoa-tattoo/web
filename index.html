Samoan Tattoo

MALU

The Malu is the traditional Samoan tattoo for women. It is a sacred and cultural symbol that represents a womans role in her family and community. The Malu usually covers the thighs and sometimes extends to the knees. It is made up of delicate, fine patterns and is done by a tufuga ta tatau (the tattoo master). In Samoan culture, wearing the Malu shows that a woman is ready to serve, lead with respect, and carry her cultural responsibilities with pride. It is not just a tattoo, it is an honor and a deep connection to heritage and identity.
