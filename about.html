This complex and detailed design resembles a Pe'a or Malofie, the tradition male tattoo that extends from the waist to the knees.

It inbcludes motifs such as:

Spearheads(niho niu)-Representing courage and warrior spirit.

Waves (galu)-Symbolizing life's journey and connection to the ocean.

Paths(ala)-Indicating direction, purpose, and ancestral paths.

This symmetrical motif is often seen in the chest or upper back region and may resemble the malu, the traditional female tattoo.

It symbolizes:

Protection and balance-The pointed edges and symmetry reflect harmony and shielding from harm.

Central flower or petal shapes-Representing femininity, spirituality, or connection to nature.

This horizontal band design is a classic tattoo band worn around the arm or leg.

It includes:

Geometric shapes-Representing structure, discipline, and unity.

Repetitive patterns-Signifying community, tradition, and the cycles of life.

Top Pattern: Full Sleeve or Body Panel

Middle Pattern: Chest or Back Symbol

Bottom Pattern: Arm or Leg Band
